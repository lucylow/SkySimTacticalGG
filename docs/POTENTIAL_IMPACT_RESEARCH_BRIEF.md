## 5) How to Measure Impact — KPIs & Evaluation Plan

### Primary KPIs (Quantitative)

- Win % change (or outcome probability measures like xG differential)
- Injury incidence per 1,000 hours (and days lost)
- Time to competency for a target skill (e.g., decision latency)
- Coach/analyst time saved (hours/week)
- LTV / ROI metrics if monetized to teams (cost saved per injury avoided, incremental revenue per win)

### Secondary KPIs (Qualitative)

- Coach satisfaction & adoption rates (surveys)
- Player perceived usefulness (surveys)
- Explainability: fraction of automated recommendations accepted/overridden

### Evaluation Design for Pilot

- **Pre/post design** + matched control where possible (e.g., compare with similar teams/players not using the system)
- Track metrics weekly; perform statistical tests for changes (t-tests, regression controlling for confounders)
- **Minimum pilot length:** 8–12 weeks for skill changes; 6–12 months to show injury and competitive outcomes reliably

## 6) Practical Pilot Plan (Recommended)

1. **Select pilot cohort** — 1 professional team OR 3 collegiate teams OR 6 youth/academy squads. Include coaching and medical staff.

2. **Baseline measurement (4–8 weeks)** — Collect historical and current data (movement, events, outcomes).

3. **Deploy micro-analytics suite** — Feed in tracking + event data; deliver weekly micro-insight reports and an in-app "action card" for coaches.

4. **Operationalize actions** — Coaches implement 1–3 recommended adjustments per week (tactical tweak, workload change, targeted drill).

5. **Monitor & adapt (ongoing)** — Track KPIs weekly; adjust detection thresholds, explainability templates; maintain human-in-loop review.

6. **Evaluation (after 3 / 6 / 12 months)** — Measure KPI deltas vs baseline and controls; produce ROI estimate.

## 9) Risks & Mitigations
